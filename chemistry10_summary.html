CHAPTER 9: CHEMICAL EQUILIBRIUM

Reversible Reactions

Reactions that go both ways. Equilibrium is when the forward rate equals the reverse rate.

Law of Mass Action

Rate is proportional to the product of molar concentrations of reactants.

K\u2092 = [Products] / [Reactants]

Importance of K\u2092

  • Predicting direction: Q\u2092 \u003c K\u2092 (Forward), Q\u2092 \u003e K\u2092 (Reverse).
  • Predicting extent: Large K\u2092 means reaction is almost complete.

CHAPTER 10: ACIDS, BASES AND SALTS

Concepts

  • Arrhenius: Acids give H\u207a, Bases give OH\u207b in water.
  • Bronsted-Lowry: Acids are proton donors, Bases are proton acceptors.
  • Lewis: Acids are electron-pair acceptors, Bases are electron-pair donors.

pH Scale

pH = -log[H\u207a]. Range is 0-14. Neutral is 7.

Salts

Formed by neutralization of acid and base. Types: Normal, Acidic, Basic.

CHAPTER 11: ORGANIC CHEMISTRY

Organic Compounds

Compounds of carbon (except CO, CO\u2082, carbonates). Characterized by catenation (carbon-carbon bonding).

Functional Groups

  • Alcohol: -OH
  • Aldehyde: -CHO
  • Ketone: \u003eC=O
  • Carboxylic Acid: -COOH

CHAPTER 12: HYDROCARBONS

Alkanes (Saturated)

Single bonds. General formula: C\u2099H\u2082\u2099\u208a\u2082. Undergo substitution reactions.

Alkenes (Unsaturated)

Double bond. General formula: C\u2099H\u2082\u2099. Undergo addition reactions.

Alkynes (Unsaturated)

Triple bond. General formula: C\u2099H\u2082\u2099\u208b\u2082.

CHAPTER 13: BIOCHEMISTRY

Carbohydrates

Monosaccharides (Glucose), Oligosaccharides (Sucrose), Polysaccharides (Starch, Cellulose).

Proteins

Polymers of amino acids. Essential for growth and repair.

Lipids & Vitamins

Vitamins are either Fat-soluble (A, D, E, K) or Water-soluble (B-complex, C).

CHAPTER 14: ATMOSPHERE

Layers

Troposphere (0-12km, weather), Stratosphere (12-50km, Ozone layer), Mesosphere, Thermosphere.

Pollutants

  • Primary: CO, CO\u2082, SO\u2082, NO\u2093.
  • Secondary: H\u2082SO\u2084, HNO\u2083 (Acid rain).

Greenhouse Effect: Global warming due to CO\u2082 trapping heat.

CHAPTER 15: WATER

Properties

Universal solvent due to polarity and hydrogen bonding.

Hardness of Water

  • Temporary: Due to bicarbonates of Ca and Mg. Removed by boiling or Clark's method.
  • Permanent: Due to chlorides and sulfates of Ca and Mg. Removed by washing soda or Ion-exchange resins.

CHAPTER 16: CHEMICAL INDUSTRIES

Metallurgy

Process of extracting metals from ores. Steps: Crushing, Concentration, Extraction, Refining.

Solvay Process

Manufacture of Sodium Carbonate (Washing Soda) using Ammonia and Brine.

Urea

Fertilizer made from Ammonia and CO\u2082.
