Biology Class 9 Notes

CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION TO BIOLOGY

Definition and Branches

Biology: The scientific study of life. It comes from Greek words 'Bios' (life) and 'Logos' (thought/reasoning).

  • Zoology: Study of animals.
  • Botany: Study of plants.
  • Microbiology: Study of microorganisms (bacteria, etc.).
  • Morphology: Study of form and internal structures.
  • Physiology: Study of functions of living parts.
  • Genetics: Study of genes and inheritance.

Relationship with Other Sciences

  • Biophysics: Principles of physics in biological processes (e.g., lever system in limbs).
  • Biochemistry: Chemistry of different compounds and processes in living organisms.
  • Biometry: Application of mathematics and statistics in biology.

Levels of Organization

Subatomic particles → Atoms → Molecules → Organelles → Cells → Tissues → Organs → Organ Systems → Organism → Population → Community → Biosphere.

CHAPTER 2: SOLVING A BIOLOGICAL PROBLEM

Biological Method

The scientific method used to solve biological problems. Steps include:

  1. Recognition of biological problem.
  2. Observations: Can be qualitative (descriptive) or quantitative (measurable).
  3. Hypothesis: A tentative explanation of observations.
  4. Deduction: Logical consequences of a hypothesis.
  5. Experimentation: Testing the hypothesis.
  6. Summarization of results.
  7. Reporting of results.

Example: Study of Malaria. Observations led to the hypothesis that 'Plasmodium is the cause of malaria'.

CHAPTER 3: BIODIVERSITY

Classification

Biodiversity: The variety of organisms present in different ecosystems.

Aims: To determine similarities and differences among organisms for easy study.

Taxonomic Hierarchy: Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species.

Five Kingdom System

  • Monera: Prokaryotic organisms (Bacteria).
  • Protista: Eukaryotic unicellular organisms.
  • Fungi: Eukaryotic absorptive heterotrophs.
  • Plantae: Eukaryotic multicellular autotrophs.
  • Animalia: Eukaryotic multicellular ingestive heterotrophs.

Conservation

Threats like deforestation and hunting lead to extinction. Examples of endangered species in Pakistan: Indus Dolphin, Houbara Bustard.

CHAPTER 4: CELLS AND TISSUES

Microscopy

  • Light Microscope: Uses visible light. Magnification up to 1500X.
  • Electron Microscope: Uses beam of electrons. High resolution.

Cell Structure

  • Cell Wall: Outer layer in plants, fungi, and bacteria.
  • Cell Membrane: Semi-permeable boundary of cell.
  • Nucleus: Control center containing genetic material.
  • Organelles: Mitochondria (Powerhouse), Ribosomes (Protein synthesis), Chloroplasts (Photosynthesis).

Tissues

  • Animal Tissues: Epithelial, Connective, Muscular, Nervous.
  • Plant Tissues: Meristematic, Permanent (Simple and Compound).

CHAPTER 5: CELL CYCLE

Phases of Cell Cycle

  • Interphase: Growth and DNA replication (G1, S, G2 phases).
  • M Phase (Mitotic Phase): Division of cell.

Mitosis

Division of cell into two daughter cells with the same number of chromosomes. Crucial for growth, repair, and asexual reproduction.

Meiosis

Reduction division where chromosomes are halved. Occurs during gamete formation. Leads to genetic variation through crossing over.

Apoptosis & Necrosis

  • Apoptosis: Programmed cell death (useful).
  • Necrosis: Accidental cell death due to injury or disease (harmful).

CHAPTER 6: ENZYMES

Characteristics

Enzymes: Biological catalysts (mostly proteins) that speed up chemical reactions without being consumed.

  • High specificity for substrates.
  • Affected by temperature and pH.
  • Require Cofactors (non-protein parts) for activity.

Mechanism

Lock and Key Model: Specific enzyme shape for a specific substrate.

Induced Fit Model: Active site changes shape slightly to fit substrate.

CHAPTER 7: BIOENERGETICS

Photosynthesis

Process by which plants make food using CO2, water, and sunlight in chloroplasts.

  • Light Reaction: Occurs in thylakoids, produces ATP and NADPH.
  • Dark Reaction (Calvin Cycle): Occurs in stroma, produces glucose.

Respiration

Breakdown of glucose to release energy (ATP).

  • Aerobic Respiration: Uses oxygen, yields more energy.
  • Anaerobic Respiration: Without oxygen (Fermentation).

CHAPTER 8: NUTRITION

Components of Food

  • Carbohydrates: Primary source of energy.
  • Proteins: Build and repair body tissues.
  • Lipids: Energy storage and insulation.
  • Vitamins: Essential for metabolic functions.

Human Digestive System

Steps: Ingestion → Digestion → Absorption → Assimilation → Defecation.

Organs: Mouth, Esophagus, Stomach, Small Intestine (Absorption), Large Intestine, Liver and Pancreas (Glands).

CHAPTER 9: TRANSPORT

Transport in Plants

  • Xylem: Transports water and minerals (Transpiration pull).
  • Phloem: Transports prepared food (Translocation).

Transport in Humans

  • Blood: Composed of Plasma, RBCs (Carry oxygen), WBCs (Immunity), Platelets (Clotting).
  • Heart: Double pump that circulates blood through Pulmonary and Systemic circuits.
  • Blood Vessels: Arteries (Away from heart), Veins (Towards heart), Capillaries (Exchange).
