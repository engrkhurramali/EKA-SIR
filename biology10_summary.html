Biology Class 10 Notes

CHAPTER 10: GASEOUS EXCHANGE

10.1 Gaseous Exchange in Plants

Occurs through stomata in leaves, lenticels in stems, and general surface of roots.

10.2 Human Respiratory System

Air passes through: Nasal cavity → Pharynx → Larynx → Trachea → Bronchi → Bronchioles → Alveoli (site of exchange).

10.3 Respiratory Disorders

Bronchitis, Emphysema, Pneumonia, Asthma, Lung Cancer.

CHAPTER 11: HOMEOSTASIS

11.1 Definition

Maintenance of internal body conditions at equilibrium despite changes in the external environment.

11.2 Osmoregulation

Maintenance of water and salt balance in body fluids.

11.3 Human Urinary System

Kidneys, Ureters, Urinary Bladder, Urethra. The functional unit is the Nephron.

CHAPTER 12: COORDINATION AND CONTROL

12.1 Coordination Types

Nervous Coordination: Rapid, electrical signals via neurons. Chemical Coordination: Slower, chemical signals via hormones.

12.2 Human Brain

Divided into Forebrain (Cerebrum, Thalamus, Hypothalamus), Midbrain, and Hindbrain (Cerebellum, Pons, Medulla Oblongata).

12.3 Reflex Action

Immediate and involuntary response to a stimulus. The pathway is called a Reflex Arc.

12.4 Endocrine System

Endocrine glands secrete hormones directly into blood (e.g., Insulin for glucose, Adrenaline for emergency).

CHAPTER 13: SUPPORT AND MOVEMENT

13.1 Human Skeleton

Provides support, protection, and movement. Consists of 206 bones divided into Axial and Appendicular skeletons.

13.2 Bone and Cartilage

Bone: Hard, calcified connective tissue. Cartilage: Flexible and soft connective tissue found at joints.

13.3 Joints

Points where bones meet. Types include Hinge joints (elbow) and Ball-and-socket joints (shoulder).

13.4 Muscles and Movement

Skeletal muscles work in pairs called antagonists (e.g., Biceps and Triceps).

CHAPTER 14: REPRODUCTION

14.1 Asexual Reproduction

Involves one parent. Types: Binary fission, Budding, Spore formation, and Vegetative propagation.

14.2 Sexual Reproduction in Plants

Involves flowers. Pollination is the transfer of pollen grains. Fertilization leads to seed formation.

14.3 Human Reproduction

Involves the production of gametes (Sperm and Egg). Fertilization results in a Zygote.

CHAPTER 15: INHERITANCE

15.1 Genetics

Study of heredity. Genes are segments of DNA located on chromosomes that carry traits.

15.2 Mendel's Laws

Law of Segregation: Alleles separate during gamete formation. Law of Independent Assortment: Different traits are inherited independently.

15.3 DNA and Variations

DNA is a double helix. Variations are differences among individuals of the same species.

CHAPTER 16: MAN AND HIS ENVIRONMENT

16.1 Ecosystem

A community of living organisms interacting with their physical environment.

16.2 Flow of Energy

Energy flows from Producers (plants) to Consumers (animals). Food chains and webs illustrate this.

16.3 Biogeochemical Cycles

Cycles like Carbon and Nitrogen cycles ensure the recycling of nutrients in nature.

16.4 Ecological Issues

Pollution, Deforestation, Global Warming, and the Greenhouse Effect.

CHAPTER 17: BIOTECHNOLOGY

17.1 Introduction

The use of living organisms or biological systems to develop products for human benefit.

17.2 Fermentation

Process using microbes to produce food (yogurt, bread) and industrial products (alcohol, vitamins).

17.3 Genetic Engineering

Technique of altering the DNA of an organism to introduce desirable traits.

17.4 Single Cell Protein

Protein-rich food produced by microorganisms like bacteria, algae, or fungi.

CHAPTER 18: PHARMACOLOGY

18.1 Medicinal Drugs

Used to treat or prevent diseases. Sources include plants, animals, minerals, and synthetics.

18.2 Addictive Drugs

Drugs that cause physical or mental dependence, such as Narcotics and Sedatives.

18.3 Antibiotics and Vaccines

Antibiotics: Kill or inhibit bacteria. Vaccines: Stimulate immunity against specific diseases.
